## Why FDS0 exists

FDS produces reviewed work: code, documents, presentations, and
decisions; across multiple workstreams. When a new member joins
without a shared baseline, the first weeks are spent on recurring
questions: how do we publish a document, how do we make a formal
declaration, how do we collaborate on shared work, what does our
LaTeX template expect. FDS0 exists so that every new member learns
these things once, in a structured order, with a one-to-one mentor
who adapts the pace to the member's background.

FDS0 is also where new members meet the FDS workflow itself: identity
and privacy rules, the motivation behind formal declarations, how
reviewed work moves through Git and DocServer, and how AI agents fit
safely into FDS contribution. None of this is intuitive on day one;
the curriculum is designed so that no member has to reverse-engineer
it from fragments.

## Structure

FDS0 is a flexible paced, mentor-based initiation program. Each member is
paired with a one-to-one mentor who runs the weekly session, adapts
the assignment to the member's background, and signs off the
completion checklist. The curriculum has thirteen seminars, a midterm
checkpoint at the end of Week 2, and a final project in Week 4.

The following topics is covered:

1. Nature of FDS
2. MyFDS Account and Identity Privacy
3. Motivation of Rules and Regulations
4. Linux or Unix Basics
5. CLI and Markdown
6. DocServer
7. Git Basics
8. GitHub Cooperation
9. Logic Basics
10. LaTeX Basics
11. LaTeX Template FDS
12. AI Agents Installation and Access
13. AI Agents Prompting and Review

Plus the **Midterm Checkpoint** (Basic FDS Workflow) and the
**Final Project** (FDS First Contribution).

## Waivers

A new member may waive specific topics if they can already
demonstrate the relevant skill to their mentor's satisfaction.
Waivers are granted **per-seminar**, not in bulk; a waived seminar
still appears in the member's audit trail with a short note from the
mentor explaining the basis for the waiver.

The self-assessment form at
[`Seminar_01-Nature_of_FDS/Self_Assessment.pdf`](Seminar_01-Nature_of_FDS/Self_Assessment.pdf)
helps the mentor and the new member decide which seminars to move
through faster and which deserve a slower walkthrough. The form is
filled in before the Week 1 session.

Two items may not be waived in any case:

- **Seminar 02 (MyFDS Account and Identity Privacy).** Identity
  decisions made here propagate to every later submission and must
  be reviewed with a mentor.
- **The Final Project.** Every member produces one reviewed real
  contribution to FDS before being recognized as having completed
  FDS0.

## Completion

A new member completes FDS0 when:

1. all non-waived seminar completion checklists are signed off by
   the mentor;
2. the midterm checkpoint has been passed; and
3. the final project has been reviewed and accepted.

The mentor records the completion in the member's FDS audit trail.
Completion carries no formal title; what matters is that the member
is now operating in the standard FDS workflow.

## Mentor eligibility

Per the FDS Charter, any member of Subordinate Manager rank or
higher may serve as a mentor (Articles 17 and 18). FDS members come
from multiple regions; the mentor should be able to explain
difficult parts in a language the member is comfortable with.
